The area is known for its diversity of birds and attract tourists who have a keen interest in ornithology. It focuses on the area around the villages Metodievo and General Kolevo, continuing the municipality General Toshevo. The area is one of the few pastures of semi-natural steppe vegetation remaining in Dobrogea. In winter and spring temporarily flooded areas are formed, which in rainy years remain until the end of June. These sunrise areas used by waterfowl nesting and feeding in winter. Among them there are leading large white goose, lapwing and red-footed falcon which is endangered. The area was designated by BirdLife as important in terms of ornithological.
